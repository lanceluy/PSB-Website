Bonsai is the discipline of cultivating miniature trees that mirror the grandeur of nature in its full scale. Each tree is a conversation between the artist's vision and the plant's own will to grow — a negotiation that unfolds over decades.

Our Foundation

Three tenets of the practice

一
Kanso · 簡素

Simplicity and elimination of clutter. A bonsai should express its essence without ornamentation — each element deliberate, nothing accidental. True beauty is found in what is withheld as much as what is shown.

二
Ma · 間

Negative space as presence. The air between branches is not emptiness — it is breath. How a tree fills its space, and the silence it commands around itself, defines its character as much as its foliage.

三
Fukinsei · 不均整

Asymmetry as a pathway to harmony. Nature does not produce perfect symmetry; neither should the bonsai artist pursue it. The irregular, the crooked, the unexpected — these are what give a tree its soul.

The Year's Rhythm

Bonsai through the seasons

🌸
Spring

The season of vigour and new growth. Trees burst with energy; this is the time for repotting, first pruning, and careful wiring of pliable new branches.

Repot · Wire · Prune
☀️
Summer

Deep watering, shade management, and pinching keep the canopy dense. Watch for pests and celebrate the full, lush silhouette of a well-fed tree.

Water · Pinch · Protect
🍂
Autumn

The tree withdraws energy to its roots. Deciduous species blaze before bare branches reveal the bones — the most honest moment in a bonsai's year.

Fertilize · Style · Admire
❄️
Winter

Rest. Shelter trees from frost while allowing dormancy. A quiet season for study, sketching future shapes, and attending to tools and stands.

Rest · Study · Sharpen
